## Block overview

You have 7 weeks to race day. The goal isn't to build a new engine—it's to sharpen what you have and eliminate the weaknesses costing you minutes. Your stations are already sub-75 pace. The race will be won or lost on the runs and your two problem stations.

### Primary objectives

1. Eliminate the run fade. You're losing 60+ seconds between your first and last kilometer. We'll train your body to hold pace when pre-fatigued through structured run-after-work sessions and back-half loaded threshold runs.

2. Build burpee and wall ball capacity. These aren't cardio problems—they're muscular endurance and skill problems. High-frequency, moderate-volume exposure will make them feel routine, not desperate.

3. Extend threshold durability. Your HR spikes on sets 4-5 of longer threshold work. We'll progressively extend your ability to hold 8.0 mph without drift through targeted interval structures.

4. Lock in core stability. Your lower back history and foot slap under fatigue point to core giving out before legs do. Every strength day includes dedicated core work—non-negotiable.

5. Race simulation and pacing. Two mock races (Weeks 9 and 10) will let you practice pacing strategy and identify any remaining holes before taper.

### Weekly structure

- **Monday:** Recovery (easy bike or walk + mobility)
- **Tuesday:** Easy Z2 run (5-6 mi) + Strength A (squat/lunge focus, core)
- **Wednesday:** Threshold run with fatigue component
- **Thursday:** Machine threshold (row/ski emphasis on pulling and pressing endurance)
- **Friday:** Key run (intervals or tempo)
- **Saturday:** Long run + station skill work
- **Sunday:** Race simulation (run + station combinations)

## Week 5: April 14–20

**Goal:** Establish new structure, introduce run-after-fatigue work, begin wall ball/burpee frequency.

**Monday 4/14 – Recovery**  
40 min C2 bike Z1-Z2 at 90 rpm, 130-150w. 10 min mobility focused on hips and ankles.

**Tuesday 4/15 – Easy Run + Strength A**  
Run: 5 mi easy Z2 (10:30-11:00/mi pace, HR under 150).  
Strength: Back squat 4x6 @ 165 lbs, reverse deficit lunge 3x8 each leg @ 95 lbs, Romanian deadlift 3x10 @ 135 lbs, dead bug 3x12 each side, pallof press 3x10 each side, plank 3x45 sec.

**Wednesday 4/16 – Threshold Run**  
Warmup: 1 mi easy.  
Main: 4x5 min @ 8.0 mph with 2 min jog recovery. Focus on holding pace through set 4 without HR exceeding 175.  
Cooldown: 1 mi easy.  
Total: ~5.5 mi.

**Thursday 4/17 – Machine Threshold**  
C2 Row: 4x1000m @ 1:55-2:00 pace with 2 min rest.  
Then: 3x15 wall balls, focusing on smooth rhythm and consistent catch height. Rest 60 sec between sets.

**Friday 4/18 – Key Run (Intervals)**  
Warmup: 1 mi easy.  
Main: 5x1 km @ 7:20-7:30/mi pace with 90 sec jog recovery.  
Cooldown: 0.5 mi easy.  
Total: ~5 mi.

**Saturday 4/19 – Long Run + Station Work**  
Run: 8 mi Z2 with last 2 mi at tempo effort (8:45-9:00/mi).  
Station work: 5x20 wall balls, 3x8 burpee broad jumps. Focus on pacing and form, not speed. Rest as needed.

**Sunday 4/20 – Race Simulation**  
5 rounds for quality (not time):  
1 km run @ 8:00-8:15/mi pace  
20 wall balls  
Rest 90 sec between rounds.  
Focus: Hold run pace consistent across all 5 rounds. Note any fade.

## Week 6: April 21–27

**Goal:** Increase threshold volume, add burpee density, introduce sled work to maintain strengths.

**Monday 4/21 – Recovery**  
35 min easy walk or Z1 bike. 15 min mobility focusing on thoracic spine and hip flexors.

**Tuesday 4/22 – Easy Run + Strength A**  
Run: 5.5 mi easy Z2.  
Strength: Front squat 4x5 @ 145 lbs, walking lunge 3x12 each leg @ 95 lbs (barbell), single-leg Romanian deadlift 3x8 each @ 40 lb dumbbell, bird dog 3x10 each side, side plank 3x30 sec each side, hollow hold 3x30 sec.

**Wednesday 4/23 – Threshold Run**  
Warmup: 1 mi easy.  
Main: 2x10 min @ 8.0 mph with 3 min jog recovery. Goal is to hold pace without cardiac drift—if HR exceeds 178 in the back half, note when it happens.  
Cooldown: 1 mi easy.  
Total: ~6 mi.

**Thursday 4/24 – Machine Threshold**  
SkiErg: 5x500m @ 1:45-1:50 pace with 90 sec rest. Focus on controlled breathing and lat engagement—this builds the pressing endurance that carries over to wall balls.  
Then: 4x10 burpee broad jumps. Rest 90 sec between sets. Prioritize consistent jump distance over speed.

**Friday 4/25 – Key Run (Tempo)**  
Warmup: 1 mi easy.  
Main: 25 min continuous @ 7:45-8:00/mi pace (treadmill 7.5-7.7 mph). No walk breaks. This is a race-pace effort for your middle kilometers.  
Cooldown: 1 mi easy.  
Total: ~5.5 mi.

**Saturday 4/26 – Long Run + Station Work**  
Run: 9 mi Z2, final 2.5 mi at moderate tempo (8:30-8:45/mi).  
Station work (at neighbor's gym): Sled push 4x50m @ race weight, sled pull 4x50m @ race weight. Keep these crisp—maintenance work, not building. Then 3x20 wall balls.

**Sunday 4/27 – Race Simulation**  
4 rounds for quality:  
1 km run @ 7:50-8:00/mi pace  
15 burpee broad jumps  
20 wall balls  
Rest 2 min between rounds.  
Track your 4th round run split versus your 1st. Target is less than 10 seconds difference.
